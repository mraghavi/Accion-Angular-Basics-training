What Are Angular Structural Directives?

Angular structural directives change the structure of the DOM. They can add, remove, or replace elements, and are prefixed with an asterisk (*).

Three standard structural directives in Angular:

  • *ngIf – Conditionally includes a template based on a Boolean expression.
  • *ngFor – Iterates over an array.

How to Use the *ngIf Directive

The *ngIf directive displays or removes an element based on a condition, similar to if-else statements in other languages.

If the expression is false, *ngIf removes the element; if true, it adds the element to the DOM.

Example of *ngIf:


        <div *ngIf="isBoxVisible"></div>
        <div class="button-group">
          <button class="btn" (click)="onBox1()">On</button>
          <button class="btn" (click)="offBox1()">Off</button>
          <button class="btn" (click)="toggle()">Toggle</button>
        </div>

When Should You Use Angular Structural Directives?

Use structural directives to add or remove elements from the DOM, change styles, add event listeners, or create new elements.

In short, whenever you need to manipulate the DOM, consider using a structural directive.
